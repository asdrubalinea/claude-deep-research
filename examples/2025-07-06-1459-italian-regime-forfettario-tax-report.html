1. Overview and Executive Summary

Key Takeaway

The Italian Regime Forfettario is a highly favorable flat-rate tax system offering 15% tax rate (5% for new businesses) with complete VAT exemption and simplified administration for qualifying small businesses and freelancers.

What is the Regime Forfettario?

The Regime Forfettario is Italy's simplified flat-rate tax system, introduced by Law 190/2014 and effective since January 1, 2015. Designed specifically for small businesses and freelancers, it replaces the complex progressive tax system with a single flat rate while eliminating VAT obligations entirely.

Who Benefits Most?

  • New Businesses: 5% tax rate for the first 5 years
  • Service Professionals: Consultants, designers, freelancers
  • Small Retailers: Competitive pricing without VAT
  • Solo Entrepreneurs: Simplified administration and compliance

Core Advantages

  • Low Tax Rates: 15% standard, 5% for startups vs 23-43% ordinary rates
  • No VAT: Complete exemption from VAT registration and payments
  • Simplified Accounting: No complex bookkeeping requirements
  • Reduced Costs: Accounting services approximately 50% cheaper
  • Competitive Pricing: Can offer lower prices without VAT

2. Legal Structure and Framework

Legislative Foundation

Governing Law: Law 190/2014 (2015 Stability Law), Articles 54-89
Effective Date: January 1, 2015
Purpose: Simplified taxation replacing all previous preferential regimes

Legal Scope and Eligibility

Who Can Use It

  • Natural persons only (individuals)
  • Business, artistic, or professional activities
  • Solo practitioners and freelancers

Who Cannot Use It

  • Corporations, partnerships, or associations
  • Cooperative societies
  • Joint-stock companies

Tax Mechanism

The regime operates on a simplified calculation:

Step Calculation Example
1. Revenue Total annual revenue €50,000
2. Apply Coefficient Revenue × ATECO Coefficient €50,000 × 78% = €39,000
3. Calculate Tax Taxable Income × 15% (or 5%) €39,000 × 15% = €5,850

What It Replaces

The Regime Forfettario substitutes multiple taxes:

  • IRPEF: Personal income tax
  • Regional and Municipal Surcharges
  • IRAP: Regional production tax
  • VAT: Value-added tax obligations

Business Structure Requirements

  • Former Employer Rule: Maximum 50% of revenue from former employers

Social security varies by business type:

  • Professional Orders: Additional professional fund contributions may apply

5. Forfettario vs Ordinary Taxation

✓ Forfettario Advantages

  • Lower Tax Rate: 15% vs 23-43% progressive
  • Startup Benefit: 5% for first 5 years
  • No VAT: Competitive pricing advantage
  • Simple Admin: No complex bookkeeping
  • Cost Savings: 50% cheaper accounting
  • Social Benefits: 35% INPS reduction available
  • No Audits: Excluded from ISA audits

⚠ Forfettario Limitations

  • No Deductions: Cannot deduct actual expenses
  • No VAT Recovery: Cannot recover VAT on purchases
  • Revenue Caps: €85,000 annual limit
  • Employee Limits: €20,000 employee cost cap
  • Growth Penalty: Major tax increase when exceeding limits
  • Stamp Duty: €2 stamp on invoices >€77.47

Financial Impact Comparison

Real-World Example: IT Technician (€35,000 income)

Tax Regime Net Income Difference
Regime Forfettario €23,286 +€6,000 (35% higher)
Ordinary Taxation €17,286 Base comparison

⚠ Cliff Effect Warning

Revenue Transition Impact:

  • €85,000 in Forfettario: Substantial net income
  • €86,000 in Ordinary: Approximately €23,000 less net income
  • Lesson: Massive penalty for small revenue increase

Business Suitability Matrix

Best for Forfettario:

  • Service Businesses: Low physical expenses, high margins
  • Freelancers/Consultants: Individual expertise-based work
  • B2C Businesses: Benefit from no VAT pricing advantage
  • Startups: 5% rate for first 5 years
  • Solo Practitioners: Simplified administration appeals

Best for Ordinary Taxation:

  • High-Expense Businesses: Can deduct significant costs
  • Equipment-Intensive: Benefit from actual expense deductions
  • Large Teams: No employee cost restrictions
  • B2B Businesses: Clients can recover VAT
  • High-Growth: No revenue limitations

Simplified Invoicing Extension

Can issue simplified invoices beyond €400 threshold (previously limited)

2024 Major Changes

Electronic Invoicing Mandatory

Effective: January 1, 2024
Requirement: All forfettario taxpayers must use XML format through SDI system
Impact: Modernized invoice processing and government tracking

Historical Revenue Limit Evolution

Legislative Trend Analysis

The consistent increases in revenue limits demonstrate the Italian government's commitment to supporting small businesses and expanding access to the regime. This trend suggests potential for further increases in future budget laws.

Mixed Contract Regulations

New for 2025: Clarified rules for using forfettario alongside employment contracts:

  • No overlap in work object, performance methods, or working hours

8. Compliance Requirements

Electronic Invoicing (Mandatory 2024+)

Invoice Requirements

  • Format: XML format through SDI system
  • SDI Code: 7-digit code required
  • VAT Notice: Must include "Operation not subject to VAT pursuant to Article 1, paragraphs 54-89 of Italian Law no. 190/2014"
  • Stamp Duty: €2 stamp for invoices exceeding €77.47

Simplified Administrative Obligations

What You DON'T Need to Do:

  • ❌ Keep detailed accounting registers
  • ❌ File quarterly VAT returns
  • ❌ Register purchase and sales invoices
  • ❌ Apply for VAT registration
  • ❌ Calculate and pay VAT
  • ❌ Submit Spesometro reports
  • ❌ Deal with ISA audits

What You DO Need to Do:

  • ✓ Issue electronic invoices for all services/products
  • ✓ Maintain copies of all issued invoices
  • ✓ Track revenue to ensure compliance with limits
  • ✓ File annual tax declaration
  • ✓ Pay social security contributions
  • ✓ Monitor eligibility criteria annually

Revenue Monitoring Requirements

Critical Monitoring Points

  • Monthly Revenue Tracking: Monitor progress toward €85,000 limit
  • Employee Cost Tracking: Ensure under €20,000 annually
  • Employment Income: Verify stays under €35,000 (2025)
  • Former Employer Revenue: Maximum 50% from previous employers
  • Business Ownership: Maintain under 25% ownership in similar businesses

Social Security Compliance

Registration Requirements:

  • Separate INPS Management: For freelancers without professional orders
  • Artisan/Retailer Category: For qualifying businesses
  • Professional Orders: If applicable to your profession

Payment Obligations:

  • Quarterly Payments: Follow standard INPS schedule
  • Annual Reconciliation: Based on actual taxable income
  • Contribution Rates: 26.23% for separate management (2023)

2025 Simplified Invoicing Extensions

Simplified Invoices: Can now issue beyond €400 threshold

Benefit: Reduced administrative burden for small transactions
